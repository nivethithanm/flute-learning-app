01 · Flute basics

Five steps to your first clear note.

Tap each step to learn it. No theory yet — just body, breath, and bamboo.

01

Parts of the flute

The blow hole is near the closed end — you blow across it, not into it. Finger holes open and close to change pitch. The open end is where sound radiates out.

💡 Bansuri has 6 holes · Carnatic venu has 7–8
02

Posture

Sit or stand tall. Shoulders down and relaxed, neck long. Hold the flute roughly horizontal or angled slightly downward. Fingers curved, sealing holes with the soft pads — not fingertips.

💡 Tension in the shoulders travels to your breath — stay loose.
03

Embouchure — your lips

Rest the blow hole on your lower lip. Make a soft "pooh" shape — not pursed like a kiss. Aim a thin, focused stream of air at the far edge of the hole.

Roll the flute slightly in or out until the tone locks in and rings clear.

💡 Chase a clear tone before chasing any note.
04

Breath control

Breathe from the belly, not the chest. Feel your abdomen expand on the inhale and slowly contract as you play. This gives you a long, even, controlled stream of air.

Plan your phrases so you inhale at natural pauses — never gasp mid-phrase.

💡 A steady belly breath = a steady tone.
05

Choosing your first flute

Start with a medium bansuri in G or A — warm tone, comfortable hole spacing, widely available. Short flutes (E, F) are shrill; long ones (B, C) strain small hands.

Daily routine: Long tones (5 min) → Sarali varisai (10 min) → one raga scale (5 min) → one thing you love (10 min).

💡 30 minutes every day beats 3 hours on weekends.

02 · Sound & waves

Air in, sound out — here's how.

Watch a breath become a pitch. The figure below blows across the hole; the air column vibrates as a standing wave; the wave radiates as sound. Drag the slider to change pitch.

—
🎵

Pitch = frequency

Faster vibration = higher note. The formula: f = v / (2L)

🌬️

Length = pitch

Open a hole → shorter column → higher note. Close all holes → longest column → lowest note.

💨

Overblow = octave

Same fingering + faster air = 2nd harmonic = one octave up. That's how the upper register works.

🎮 Build the note — click holes to open/close

Each open hole shortens the air column and raises the pitch. Try to match the target note shown in gold.

"Length" is relative — lower keys are longer flutes with a wider finger reach.

What changes when you change keys?

Nothing about the relationships between swaras — Sa to Pa is always a perfect fifth. Only the absolute pitches move. That's why notation written in swaras is portable across flutes: the same "S R G P D" plays correctly on a C flute and an A flute; it just sounds higher or lower.
